Scania Brake Pad & Disc Replacement: A Comprehensive Guide

Maintaining the braking system on your Scania truck or bus is crucial for safety and performance. This guide provides a step-by-step process for replacing brake pads and discs. Always prioritize safety and consult your Scania service manual for specific torque values and recommendations.

When to Replace Scania Brake Pads and Discs

Several factors indicate the need for brake pad and disc replacement:

  • Worn Brake Pads: Visual inspection. Look for pad thickness below minimum specifications (typically 3mm).
  • Worn Brake Discs: Measure disc thickness. Discs thinner than the minimum stamped thickness require replacement.
  • Braking Noise: Squealing, grinding, or scraping noises during braking.
  • Reduced Braking Performance: Longer stopping distances or a spongy brake pedal.
  • Uneven Braking: Vehicle pulling to one side during braking.
  • Warning Lights: Activation of the brake wear indicator light.

Tools and Materials Needed

  • New Scania brake pads and discs (ensure correct part numbers)
  • Wheel chocks
  • Jack and jack stands (rated for the vehicle's weight)
  • Socket set and wrenches (including torque wrench)
  • Brake caliper piston compression tool
  • Brake cleaner
  • Wire brush
  • Copper grease or anti-seize compound
  • Gloves and eye protection
  • Scania service manual

Step-by-Step Replacement Process

1. Safety First!

Park the vehicle on a level surface. Engage the parking brake. Chock the wheels opposite the ones being worked on. Wear gloves and eye protection.

2. Wheel Removal

Loosen the wheel nuts. Jack up the vehicle and securely support it with jack stands. Remove the wheel.

3. Caliper Removal

Locate and disconnect any brake pad wear sensor wires. Remove the caliper mounting bolts (typically located on the back of the caliper). Carefully slide the caliper off the brake disc. Support the caliper to avoid strain on the brake hose. Do NOT let it hang by the hose.

4. Brake Pad Removal

Remove the old brake pads from the caliper. Note their orientation for correct installation of the new pads.

5. Caliper Piston Retraction

Use a brake caliper piston compression tool to carefully retract the piston fully into the caliper. This is essential for installing the new, thicker brake pads. Be careful not to damage the piston or the dust boot.

6. Disc Removal

Remove the caliper mounting bracket (if necessary for disc removal). Remove the disc retaining screw (if present). Carefully remove the old brake disc. It may be seized; use penetrating oil and a hammer if needed, but avoid damaging the hub.

7. Surface Preparation

Clean the hub surface with a wire brush to remove rust and debris. Apply a thin layer of copper grease or anti-seize compound to the hub to prevent future seizure.

8. Disc Installation

Install the new brake disc, ensuring it sits flush against the hub. Secure it with the retaining screw (if applicable). Tighten to the manufacturer's specified torque.

9. Caliper Bracket Installation (if removed)

Install the caliper mounting bracket. Tighten the bolts to the manufacturer's specified torque.

10. Brake Pad Installation

Install the new brake pads into the caliper. Ensure they are correctly seated in the caliper bracket. If shims are present, ensure they are correctly positioned.

11. Caliper Installation

Carefully slide the caliper back onto the brake disc. Ensure the brake pads are correctly aligned with the disc. Install the caliper mounting bolts. Tighten the bolts to the manufacturer's specified torque. Reconnect the brake pad wear sensor wires.

12. Wheel Installation

Install the wheel. Tighten the wheel nuts to the manufacturer's specified torque in a star pattern. Remove the jack stands and lower the vehicle.

13. Repeat for Other Side

Repeat the process for the other side of the axle.

14. Brake System Bleeding (if necessary)

If you disconnected the brake hose, you will need to bleed the brake system to remove any air bubbles. Consult your Scania service manual for the proper bleeding procedure.

15. Bedding-In Procedure

Perform a bedding-in procedure for the new brake pads and discs. This involves a series of moderate braking applications to heat cycle the components and ensure optimal performance. Consult your Scania service manual for the recommended bedding-in procedure.
