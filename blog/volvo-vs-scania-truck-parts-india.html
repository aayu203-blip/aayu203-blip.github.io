Volvo vs Scania Truck Parts in India: Which Is Easier to Source?

Published: 2026-01-15 • Parts Trading Company, Mumbai

India's trucking industry runs on two giants: Volvo and Scania. Both brands dominate India's mining, construction, and long-haul freight sectors. But when a vehicle breaks down, the real question is: which brand's parts are easier to source?

Parts Availability in India

Volvo trucks have been in India since the late 1990s, and spare parts supply chains have matured significantly. The Volvo spare parts network in Mumbai, Chennai, and Delhi is well-established with both OEM and quality aftermarket options.

Scania entered the Indian market later but has grown rapidly in mining states. Scania spare parts in India have historically been harder to source locally, making suppliers like Parts Trading Company critical for same-day replacements.

What Parts Fail Most Often?

For Volvo trucks, the most frequently replaced parts are brake systems (S-cam brake adjusters, brake chambers), clutch assemblies, and air dryer filters. These are high-wear items on India's rough roads.

For Scania, the top replacement parts include fuel injectors, differential seals, and compression release valve kits—parts that deteriorate faster on Indian diesel quality.
