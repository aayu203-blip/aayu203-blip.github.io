OEM vs Aftermarket Parts: Complete Buying Guide

Published: October 15, 2025 • 10 min read

When replacing heavy equipment parts, you face a critical decision: OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) or aftermarket parts? This guide will help you make informed choices based on your specific needs and budget.

What Are OEM Parts?

OEM parts are manufactured by or for the original equipment manufacturer (Volvo, Scania, Komatsu, etc.). These parts are identical to what came with your equipment from the factory.

OEM Parts Advantages:

  • ✓ Perfect fit: Guaranteed compatibility
  • ✓ Quality assurance: Meets original specifications
  • ✓ Warranty protection: May be required for warranty coverage
  • ✓ Predictable performance: Known quality and longevity
  • ✓ Resale value: Maintains equipment value

OEM Parts Disadvantages:

  • ✗ Higher cost: Premium pricing (20-40% more)
  • ✗ Availability: May need to order, longer wait times
  • ✗ Limited sources: Fewer suppliers

What Are Aftermarket Parts?

Aftermarket parts are manufactured by third-party companies to fit and function like OEM parts. Quality varies significantly by manufacturer.

Aftermarket Parts Advantages:

  • ✓ Cost savings: 20-50% less expensive
  • ✓ Better availability: More suppliers, faster delivery
  • ✓ Competitive quality: Premium aftermarket equals OEM
  • ✓ More options: Multiple brands to choose from

⚠️ Aftermarket Parts Disadvantages:

  • ✗ Quality variation: Not all aftermarket is equal
  • ✗ Warranty concerns: May void OEM warranties
  • ✗ Fit issues: Occasional compatibility problems
  • ✗ Lifespan uncertainty: May not last as long as OEM

When to Choose OEM Parts

Opt for OEM parts in these situations:

  • Equipment under warranty: Protect your warranty coverage
  • Critical components: Engine internals, major hydraulic parts
  • Safety systems: Brakes, steering, lift mechanisms
  • High-stress applications: Mining, heavy-duty operations
  • Newer equipment: First 2-3 years of operation
  • Resale considerations: Planning to sell equipment soon

When Aftermarket Makes Sense

Quality aftermarket parts are excellent choices for:

  • Maintenance items: Filters, belts, hoses
  • Wear parts: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, undercarriage
  • Older equipment: Out of warranty
  • Budget constraints: When cost is a primary factor
  • High-volume replacements: Items changed frequently
  • Emergency situations: When OEM parts aren't immediately available

Questions to Ask Your Supplier

  1. Is this an OEM or aftermarket part?
  2. Who is the actual manufacturer?
  3. What warranty is included?
  4. Do you have quality certifications?
  5. What is the expected lifespan vs OEM?
  6. Can you provide references or reviews?
  7. What is your return policy?
