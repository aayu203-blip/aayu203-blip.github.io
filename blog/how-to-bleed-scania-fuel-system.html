Bleeding a Scania Fuel System: A Comprehensive Guide

Air in the fuel system of your Scania truck can cause a variety of problems, from rough idling and poor performance to complete engine failure. Bleeding the fuel system removes trapped air, ensuring a smooth and reliable fuel supply to the engine. This guide provides a detailed, step-by-step process for bleeding your Scania's fuel system effectively.

Why is Bleeding Necessary?

Air can enter the fuel system in several ways, including:

  • Running the fuel tank completely dry.
  • Replacing fuel filters or other fuel system components.
  • Leaks in fuel lines or connections.

Ignoring air in the system can lead to costly repairs, so it's crucial to address the issue promptly.

Step-by-Step Bleeding Procedure

Before you begin, gather the following tools:

  • Wrenches (various sizes, typically metric)
  • Collection container (for catching spilled fuel)
  • Clean rags
  • Safety glasses
  • Gloves

Step 1: Locate the Bleed Points

Scania fuel systems typically have multiple bleed points. Common locations include:

  • On top of the fuel filter housing.
  • At the fuel injection pump.
  • On the fuel lines leading to the injectors.

Refer to your Scania's service manual for the exact locations of the bleed points for your specific engine model. This is crucial as different engines have slightly different setups.

Step 2: Bleeding at the Fuel Filter

  1. Locate the bleed screw on the fuel filter housing.
  2. Place a collection container beneath the bleed screw to catch any spilled fuel.
  3. Loosen the bleed screw slightly.
  4. Operate the manual fuel priming pump (if equipped) or turn the ignition to the 'on' position (but do not start the engine) to activate the electric fuel pump. This will force fuel through the system.
  5. Allow fuel to flow from the bleed screw until all air bubbles are eliminated.
  6. Tighten the bleed screw securely.

Step 3: Bleeding at the Fuel Injection Pump

  1. Locate the bleed screw on the fuel injection pump.
  2. Place a collection container beneath the bleed screw.
  3. Loosen the bleed screw slightly.
  4. Continue operating the manual priming pump or electric fuel pump until fuel flows freely from the bleed screw without any air bubbles.
  5. Tighten the bleed screw securely.

Step 4: Bleeding at the Injector Lines (If Necessary)

If the engine still doesn't start or runs roughly after bleeding at the filter and pump, you may need to bleed the injector lines. This is generally only necessary in severe cases.

  1. Loosen the fuel line connection at each injector slightly.
  2. Crank the engine for short bursts (5-10 seconds) until fuel sprays out from each connection without air bubbles.
  3. Tighten the fuel line connections securely.

Step 5: Start the Engine

Once you have bled the fuel system at all necessary points, attempt to start the engine. It may take a few tries for the engine to catch and run smoothly. If the engine still doesn't start, re-check all bleed points and connections for leaks.

Important Safety Precautions

  • Always wear safety glasses and gloves to protect yourself from fuel spray.
  • Work in a well-ventilated area to avoid inhaling fuel fumes.
  • Clean up any spilled fuel immediately to prevent slips and fire hazards.
  • Never smoke or use open flames near the fuel system.

Troubleshooting Tips

If you continue to experience problems after bleeding the fuel system, consider the following:

  • Check for fuel leaks.
  • Inspect the fuel filter for clogs.
  • Verify the fuel pump is functioning correctly.
  • Consult a qualified Scania mechanic if you are unsure about any aspect of the bleeding procedure.
