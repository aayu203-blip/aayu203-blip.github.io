Extend your rock breaker chisel's life! Expert guide to inspection, lubrication, regrinding, and more. Keep your hydraulic hammer performing strong.

Rock Breaker Chisel Maintenance: The Key to Hydraulic Hammer Longevity

A rock breaker (or hydraulic hammer) is a powerful attachment, but its performance hinges on the health of its chisel. Neglecting chisel maintenance can lead to premature wear, reduced breaking efficiency, and even damage to the hammer itself. This comprehensive guide covers crucial aspects of chisel maintenance, ensuring optimal performance and extending the lifespan of your investment.

Chisel Inspection: Identifying Problems Early

Regular visual inspection is the first line of defense. Perform this daily, or at least after each major job.

Signs of Wear and Damage to Look For:

  • Mushrooming: Deformation at the chisel head due to repeated impact.
  • Cracking: Hairline or more significant cracks anywhere on the chisel.
  • Excessive Wear: Noticeable material loss, especially at the tip.
  • Ovality: The chisel tip becoming oval-shaped instead of round.
  • Damage to the Retainer Groove: Grooves that secure the chisel become deformed.

Any of these signs indicate the need for immediate attention, ranging from regrinding to replacement.

Chisel Lubrication: Preventing Friction and Wear

Proper lubrication is absolutely critical. Lack of lubrication generates excessive heat, leading to rapid wear, scoring, and even chisel seizure. Always use the correct grease specifically designed for hydraulic hammers. General purpose grease is insufficient.

Lubrication Best Practices:

  • Grease Type: Use a high-quality molybdenum disulfide (moly) grease recommended by your hammer manufacturer.
  • Frequency: Lubricate the chisel several times per day, especially before and after long operating periods.
  • Application: Use a grease gun to apply grease through the chisel lubrication points. Ensure grease is visible exiting the hammer around the chisel.
  • Auto-Lubrication Systems: Consider installing an automatic lubrication system for consistent and hassle-free greasing.

Incorrect greasing can cause more harm than good. Consult your hammer's manual for the specific recommendations.

Chisel Regrinding: Restoring Breaking Efficiency

As the chisel tip wears, it loses its optimal shape, reducing breaking efficiency and increasing stress on the hammer. Regrinding restores the correct profile.

Regrinding Guidelines:

  • Frequency: Regrind when the tip becomes rounded or develops an oval shape.
  • Grinding Wheel: Use a grinding wheel specifically designed for hardened steel.
  • Technique: Grind slowly and evenly, avoiding excessive heat buildup. Dip the chisel in water frequently to cool it.
  • Profile: Maintain the original chisel tip profile. Consult your hammer's manual for the recommended shape.
  • Avoid Over-Grinding: Removing too much material weakens the chisel.

If you are uncomfortable with regrinding, contact a qualified machine shop.

Chisel Replacement: Knowing When to Replace

Despite careful maintenance, chisels eventually reach the end of their lifespan. Replacing a worn or damaged chisel is essential to prevent damage to the hammer itself.

Reasons for Chisel Replacement:

  • Excessive Wear: If the chisel has been reground several times and is nearing its minimum allowable length.
  • Deep Cracks: Cracks that penetrate deeply into the chisel body.
  • Severe Deformation: Significant mushrooming or bending.
  • Damage to the Retainer Groove: If the grooves that secure the chisel are badly damaged.

Chisel Storage: Protecting Your Investment

Proper storage is crucial to prevent rust and damage when chisels are not in use.

Storage Recommendations:

  • Clean and Dry: Clean the chisel thoroughly and dry it completely before storage.
  • Apply Rust Preventative: Coat the chisel with a rust preventative oil or grease.
  • Store Horizontally: Store the chisel horizontally in a dry, covered location.
  • Avoid Direct Contact with the Ground: Use a rack or pallet to keep the chisel off the ground.

Conclusion

By following these maintenance guidelines, you can significantly extend the lifespan of your rock breaker chisels, reduce downtime, and improve the overall efficiency of your hydraulic hammer. Regular inspection, proper lubrication, timely regrinding, and smart storage are all essential components of a comprehensive chisel maintenance program.
